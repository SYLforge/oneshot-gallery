# GRADIENT PLAZA — breakdown

## The hook

Somewhere there is a shopping mall that only exists after closing, and its
PA system never got the memo about the closing part. FM 88.8 broadcasts
from the second-floor fountain court all night: escalator jazz at two,
rain in the food court at three-thirty, and announcements addressed to
shoppers who will never hear them. The page never explains this. You
arrive at 3:33 AM, the floor is already rushing toward a sunset that never
finishes, and the station assumes you stayed on purpose.

The design bet: vaporwave works as a *place*, not a filter. If the grid,
the windows, and the announcements all belong to one fictional building,
the nostalgia stops being a Tumblr moodboard and starts being somewhere
you can stand.

## Art direction

Vaporwave is the easiest aesthetic in this gallery to do badly, because
its source material was never designed — it was accumulated. So the entry
treats the classic kit (grid, sun, neon, windows) with the discipline the
originals never had: one accent (`#ff71ce` pink), one "live" color
(`#05ffa1` mint), and a hard rule that anything you must read is set in
lavender-white (15.4:1), haze (10.0:1), cyan (9.9:1), mint, or pink —
never dimmer. Violet, at 5.7:1, is legally AA and still gets demoted to
labels and chrome, because a page-long paragraph at 5.7:1 *feels* like
fog even when it passes.

Three faces, three jobs. Monoton is the neon tube over the entrance —
display sizes only, because signage is not a reading face. Gugi carries
every Hangul glyph; it sounds like a PA speaker in a tiled atrium, which
is exactly the register the Korean copy is written in. Azeret Mono is the
broadcast console: schedule, chrome, body. Both working stacks run
Latin-first with Gugi second, so Hangul falls through automatically —
one fallback-sans glyph would break the whole fiction.

## Technique 1 — the floor that rushes (perspective grid math)

The hero grid is a 2D canvas, and the whole illusion is one division. Put
the horizon at 58% of the hero's height and give every grid row a depth
`z` in "lane units". A camera at depth 0 projects that row onto the screen
at

```ts
// Hero.tsx — d: 1 at the bottom edge → 0 at the horizon
const d = NEAR / z;                       // NEAR = 0.92
const y = horizon + (h - horizon) * d;
```

Rows are spawned at integer depths and slide toward you by a shared
`offset`; a row whose `z` drops below `NEAR` has passed the camera and is
skipped. Because `d` is hyperbolic in `z`, distant rows crowd the horizon
and near rows accelerate off the bottom edge — that acceleration *is* the
perspective; no easing curve is involved. Vertical lines are even
cheaper: every lane converges on the vanishing point, so they are straight
strokes from the horizon's center to evenly spaced points below the
canvas, drawn with one vertical fade gradient reused as `strokeStyle` for
the entire fan.

The signature move is what drives `offset`. A `useScrollEnergy` hook
measures scroll *velocity* — not position — and smooths it into a 0→1
energy with an asymmetric lerp (attack 0.16, release 0.05):

```ts
// useScrollEnergy.ts — the mall notices you fast, calms down slowly
const target = Math.min(1, v / V_MAX);    // full rush at 2.4 px/ms
const k = (target > energy ? ATTACK : RELEASE) * (dt / 16.7);
energy += (target - energy) * Math.min(1, k);
```

```ts
// Hero.tsx — stroll: 0.5 lanes/s. rush: ~9× that, and the horizon flares
offset += (dt / 1000) * (BASE_SPEED + energy * RUSH_SPEED);
```

At rest the floor drifts gently on its own (so touch devices get a living
scene without hovering anything); scroll hard and the floor rushes, the
horizon glow brightens by `0.55 · energy`, and the sun's halo widens. The
sun itself is drawn as 2px horizontal slices lerping pink→violet, skipping
slices where a venetian-blind gap falls — the gaps widen toward the
horizon by construction (`period = 9 + dy · 0.55`), which is the entire
"sliced sun" recipe. Stars are particles with hashed twinkle phases whose
positions are pure functions of time, so the reduced-motion still frame is
just one honest moment of the same sky.

## Technique 2 — windows you can throw (drag physics and z-order)

The booth renders three retro-OS windows. Without JavaScript they are
ordinary sections stacked in document flow — every track and label
readable. When `.plaza-js` lands, the stage becomes a desktop
(`position: relative`, fixed height) and each window takes a spawn point
in CSS. Dragging never touches `left`/`top`: position lives in a ref and
is written as `translate3d`, so a drag costs the compositor, not layout.

Pointer capture does the heavy lifting. The title-bar grip captures its
pointer on down, so moves keep arriving even when the cursor outruns the
bar; `touch-action: none` on the grip (and only the grip) means a thumb
can drag a window while the page still scrolls everywhere else:

```ts
// useDrag.ts — release keeps a little momentum
const decay = Math.pow(FRICTION, dt / 16.7);   // 0.9 per normalized frame
vel.current.x *= decay;
vel.current.y *= decay;
if (Math.hypot(vel.current.x, vel.current.y) > STOP_SPEED) {
  rafRef.current = requestAnimationFrame(step);
}
```

Velocity is sampled from the last ~12ms of pointer travel, and on release
the window glides with exponential friction, clamped to the stage with
±16px of tuck — hitting a wall zeroes that axis's velocity, so windows
thud against edges instead of vibrating. Under reduced motion the glide is
skipped entirely: dragging still works (it is user-driven input), the
window just stops where your hand stops.

Z-order is a raise stack, not a counter. The parent keeps an array of
window ids; raising a window moves its id to the end, and each window's
`z-index` is `10 + indexOf(id)` — no ever-growing `++zIndex`, and the
render is pure state. The grip is a real button, so the same window moves
by keyboard: arrows nudge 24px (Shift, 4px; Home respawns), announced in
the grip's bilingual `aria-label`. Minimize and close are real buttons
too — closed windows dock in a taskbar with `aria-pressed`, which is what
keeps decorative chrome honest.

## Technique 3 — the smear (chromatic aberration without a filter)

The classic RGB-split is usually done with an SVG `feOffset` filter graph
or a canvas channel copy. Both put the *readable* pixels inside the
effect — get the math wrong and your heading is illegible. This entry
inverts the layering: the base glyphs never move, and the aberration is
two pseudo-element *ghosts* stacked over them:

```css
/* styles.css — ghosts ride two custom properties written by JS */
.plaza-ab::before,
.plaza-ab::after {
  content: attr(data-text) / "";     /* empty alt: silent to readers */
  position: absolute;
  inset: 0;
  opacity: var(--plaza-ghost, 0);
  mix-blend-mode: screen;            /* additive light — can only brighten */
  pointer-events: none;
}
.plaza-ab::before { color: var(--plaza-cyan); transform: translate3d(calc(var(--plaza-shift) * -1), 0, 0); }
.plaza-ab::after  { color: var(--plaza-pink); transform: translate3d(var(--plaza-shift), 0, 0); }
```

`useScrollEnergy` writes `--plaza-shift` (0–7px) and `--plaza-ghost`
(0–0.85) on the entry root from the same energy that drives the floor, so
one number keeps the whole page in sync: rush, and the headings smear
cyan-left/pink-right like a de-converged CRT; stop, and everything eases
back to crisp over the release curve. `screen` blending is the contrast
insurance — ghosts can only *add* light over the unmoving base text, so
the worst case is a brighter heading, never a dimmer one.

The PA ticker closes the loop on the same number: a JS marquee (duplicated
`aria-hidden` track, wrap by modulo of half its width, a visually-hidden
list for screen readers) whose speed is `42 + 300 · energy` px/s. Stroll
and the announcements drift by; run and the PA hurries after you.

## The contrast discipline

Numbers, because vaporwave dies without them (all on `#1a0b2e`):

- lavender-white `#efe6ff` — 15.4:1, all body copy
- mint `#05ffa1` — 14.0:1, live markers
- haze `#c7b8e0` — 10.0:1, secondary text and Korean glosses
- cyan `#01cdfe` — 9.9:1, times, buttons, focus
- pink `#ff71ce` — 7.5:1, the accent (legal even for body, spent on
  display)
- violet `#b967ff` — 5.7:1, **labels and chrome only**

Dark-on-neon is checked too: deep-purple text on the title-bar gradient
holds 7.5:1 on the pink end and 5.7:1 on the violet end; taskbar buttons
flip to void-on-violet at 6.1:1 when pressed. The hero seats its copy on a
radial scrim (`void` at 82%) so body-size text stays AA even where the
brightest grid lines pass beneath it.

## Performance notes

- Three rAF loops total (grid, ticker, visualizer), every one paused by
  IntersectionObserver when offscreen and by `visibilitychange` when the
  tab hides. The scroll-energy loop is stricter: it wakes on the first
  scroll event and parks itself once energy drains below 0.004, so an
  idle page runs zero frames.
- Canvases are DPR-capped at 2. The grid draws ~26 horizontal strokes, a
  fan of verticals under one reused gradient, ~150 star rects, and ~100
  sun slices — comfortably under a millisecond on mid hardware. The
  visualizer batches by color: all mint bars, then all pink caps — two
  `fillStyle` assignments per frame.
- Windows animate exclusively via `transform`; raising is a `z-index`
  write on state change; nothing in the entry animates a layout property.
- Custom-property writes (`--plaza-shift`, `--plaza-ghost`) happen at most
  once per frame inside the energy loop, and the properties they feed are
  transform and opacity on promoted pseudo-elements.

## Accessibility notes

- **Reduced motion:** the grid draws one composed still (a pure function
  of a hand-picked `t`, so it is a real frame, not a blank), aberration
  ghosts are removed with `content: none` plus zeroed variables, the
  ticker never starts, lamp and progress animations are stripped, and drag
  inertia is off. Dragging itself remains — it only moves when the visitor
  moves.
- **No-JS:** `.plaza-js` gates the desktop positioning, canvas, and ticker
  motion; without it the windows stack in flow over a CSS gradient sky and
  every word on the page is plainly readable.
- **Semantics:** both canvases carry `role="img"` with bilingual
  descriptions; the moving ticker track is `aria-hidden` with a
  visually-hidden list as the accessible copy; grips, minimize, close,
  taskbar, and track rows are native buttons; the playing track is
  `aria-current`; window visibility is honest `hidden`.
- **Focus:** a cyan `:focus-visible` ring with a pink halo, visible on
  the dark stage and inside the neon title bars alike.

## Remix ideas

1. **Late-night ferry terminal.** Same bones, new building: the grid
   floor becomes a dark sea with a moon path, windows become DEPARTURES.EXE
   and a tide visualizer, the PA announces sailings for passengers who
   stopped arriving in 1998. Swap pink for signal-orange, keep the
   discipline.
2. **All-night laundromat FM.** The visualizer becomes a drum-rotation
   radar, the schedule lists spin cycles as shows, and the aberration
   triggers on the *washer's* rhythm — a slow pulse every 4.2s — instead
   of scroll. Good exercise: the energy envelope stays, the input changes.
3. **Closed-circuit aquarium.** The grid inverts into a ceiling (light
   caustics drawn with the same projected-rows math upside down), windows
   become tank cameras with pan buttons, and SIGNAL LOST is the one tank
   nobody films anymore.
