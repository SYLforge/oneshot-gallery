# MINHWA 민화 — breakdown

## The hook

A minhwa is a wish painted by hand. Every motif carries meaning: the tiger
under the pine with the magpie above (호작도) is a wish for glad tidings and
protection; the peony in full bloom (모란) is a wish for prosperity and
honor; the ten things that do not age, gathered under sun and moon
(십장생), is the deepest wish of all — long life. MINHWA is a gallery of
those wishes, painted by unnamed Joseon hands for households that could not
afford a literati scroll — rough where the literati refined, generous where
they withheld, meaning first and finish second.

The design bet: minhwa has no direct competitor among award-winning sites
(the gallery's own reference doc calls this the "Korean-traditional gap").
An entry that modernizes the folk picture itself — naive, vivid, symbolic,
joyful — rather than the dancheong line (GIWA's territory) fills the gap no
Awwwards winner occupies. The page is the bright opposite of KEMURI's
austere zen.

## Art direction

Aged hanji (`#f5efe1`) is the ground; ink-black `#1a1410` (흑, 13.6:1 AAA)
carries every word that matters. The accent system is the **obangsaek
(오방색)** — the five-color cosmology of Korean decorative tradition — but
treated as a *system with roles*, not a swatch set. Each hue owns a
direction, a season, an element, and the motif it lives in:

- `jeok` `#c8362b` (적 · south/summer/fire) — the functional red: focus,
  selection, the signature stroke (4.7:1 AA)
- `cheong` `#2f6e6a` (청 · east/spring/wood) — pine, tiger's sky, fine text
  (5.0:1 AA)
- `hwang` `#d99a1f` (황 · center/soil/emperor) — the sun, longevity gold;
  **display-only** (2.6:1 on hanji, never text)
- `baek` `#fbf8ef` (백 · west/autumn/metal) — the moon, powdered-shell
  breath, card grounds
- `heuk` `#1a1410` (흑 · north/winter/water) — ink line, every functional
  glyph

Three typefaces, three voices. **Gaegu** (the naive hand-brushed village
face) is the wordmark 민화, motif titles, the seal. **Noto Serif KR** is
every Korean body glyph — the warm docent. **Fraunces** is the English
gallery-card subtitle. Korean always leads; English is the leaning italic
companion, never larger. The page reads Hangul-first.

## Technique — symbol-reveal with a stamp (char-split-reveal + spring-press)

Each minhwa motif reveals with a cheerful pop, not an austere ink-settle —
because minhwa is cheerful. A stamp (motif-stamp: opacity 0→1, scale
0.82→1 with a `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` overshoot, 760ms) lands
the painting onto its mount, and the symbol-meaning narration types itself
glyph-by-glyph underneath — a curator reading aloud. The hero tiger-magpie
gets the full treatment; the peony and longevity panels reveal the same
way at staggered offsets.

Under reduced motion every motif is already stamped and every line already
typed (no pop, no glyph cadence); without JavaScript the page is a finished
folk-painting gallery — every wish visible, every meaning captioned.

## Technique — the obangsaek navigator (a palette as a system)

The ObangsaekNavigator is the page's didactic heart. Selecting one of the
five colors sets `--minhwa-accent-live` on the root, which retints the
focus ring, the section eyebrow, and the hover washes instantly — **pigment
does not ease.** Each color's row explains its direction, season, element,
and the motif it lives in, so the palette teaches itself. This is the
obangsaek used as a cosmological color system, the way a real dancheong
painter uses it — not as decoration.

## Technique — the 인장 seal-stamp (the signature interaction)

A minhwa painter signs a finished work with a red vermillion seal — the
mark that says "this one is mine, this one is done." MINHWA turns that into
a UI confirmation pattern. The visitor presses the seal-stamp button (or,
on coarse pointers / reduced motion, it self-stamps when its panel enters
view), and a vermillion 도장 presses down onto the peony section with an
even `ease-press` descend, lands, and a 1px ink rim expands to certify the
mark. Pressing a seal is a physical act — the button carries a hand-drawn
`4px 5px 0` offset shadow as the one playful element. The seal `useSealStamp`
hook self-stamps on touch and reduced-motion so no visitor misses the
certification; on fine pointers it waits for the press, because pressing is
the point.

`bismuthIllustrious_v80` + `toonystarkKoreanWebtoonFlux` LoRA @ 0.4, 832×1216,
30 steps, CFG 7, RealESRGAN 2×, AVIF+WebP). The full recipe ships in
`image-recipe.md` and the exported graph in `workflows/minhwa.json`. The
`bismuthIllustrious_v80` row in `docs/model-licenses.md` is audited

## Accessibility notes

- **Reduced motion:** motifs land already stamped, narration types land
  whole, the seal sits pressed, the longevity scrub pins to its final
  symbol, obangsaek tint swaps remain instant (they are not motion). One
  fully-composed folk-painting gallery.
- **No-JS:** every pre-reveal state is gated behind `.minhwa-js`, added on
  mount. With JavaScript off the page is a finished gallery — every wish
  visible, every meaning captioned, the seal at rest.
- **Contrast:** heuk 13.6:1, ink-soft 6.5:1, jeok 4.7:1, cheong 5.0:1 —
  all AA or better. Hwang (2.6:1) is display/decorative only, or sits as a
  dot over heuk. Baek is never text on hanji.
- **Semantics:** each minhwa panel is a `<figure>` with a bilingual
  `<figcaption>`; the longevity symbols are an ordered list; focus is a
  2px jeok `:focus-visible` ring that retints with the obangsaek.

## Performance notes

- The only per-frame writes are two CSS custom properties
  (`--minhwa-longevity` on the sticky stage, `--minhwa-accent-live` on the
  root on selection) — both transform/opacity-derived.
- The two ambient hero cycles (sun 7.1s, moon 6.3s) share no common
  divisor, so the idle hero never visibly loops. rAF pauses offscreen and
  on `visibilitychange`.
- Reveals write `class` + `transitionDelay` imperatively from one
  IntersectionObserver — no React re-renders on scroll.
- Three AVIF illustrations (~439 KB total) under the 5120 KB cap. Zero
  dependencies; heaviest assets are three font families, Latin/CJK
  subsetting left to next/font.

## Remix ideas

1. **The wedding screen.** Replace the three motifs with a minhwa wedding
   screen (혼례폭병) — each panel a wish for the couple, the seal pressed
   to "confirm" the union.
2. **The dream book.** Each visitor "dreams" a motif (a one-click picker),
   the page generates a personalized minhwa wish and seals it — a folk-art
   fortune-telling interaction.
3. **The fold.** A real eight-panel minhwa folding screen (병풍) that
   unfolds horizontally on scroll, each panel revealing a season. The
   obangsaek navigator retints by season.
